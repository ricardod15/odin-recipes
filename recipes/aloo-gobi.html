Aloo Gobi

Description

Aloo Gobi is a classic North Indian dish that features potatoes and cauliflower as the main ingredients, cooked with spices such as turmeric, cumin, coriander, ginger, garlic, and sometimes garam masala. The turmeric gives the dish its characteristic golden-yellow color. It is commonly prepared dry but can also have a light gravy, making it versatile for pairing with roti, naan, or rice.

Ingredients

Essential ingredients

  • Potatoes
  • Cauliflower
  • Onion
  • Garlic
  • Ginger
  • Tomato
  • Vegetable oil

Essential Spices

  • Turmeric powder
  • Ground cumin
  • Red chilli powder
  • Salt

Optional

  • Fresh cilantro
  • Graram masala

Steps

  1. Prep the vegetables:

    • Peel and cube 2 medium potatoes.
    • Cut 1 small head of cauliflower into florets.
    • Finely chop 1 small onion.
    • Optional: mince 2 garlic cloves and 1 teaspoon ginger.

  2. Heat the oil:

    • In a large pan, heat 2 tablespoons of cooking oil over medium heat.

  3. Sauté aromatics:

    • Add the chopped onion (and optional garlic/ginger) and sauté until fragrant and lightly golden.

  4. Add spices:

    • Stir in ½ teaspoon turmeric and a pinch of salt.
    • Optional: ½ teaspoon cumin and ¼ teaspoon red chili powder for extra flavor.

  5. Cook the vegetables:

    • Add potatoes and cauliflower, stirring well to coat with spices.
    • Optional: add 1 chopped tomato for moisture.
    • Cover and cook for 15 to 20 minutes, stirring occasionally, until vegetables are tender.

  6. Finish and serve:

    • Taste and adjust salt if needed.
    • Optional: garnish with fresh chopped cilantro.
    • Serve hot as a simple, flavorful dish.
